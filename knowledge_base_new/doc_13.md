Barith Aldfred was a Warak of the Durenlenheart in the Topaztail. He was hired by Taeth to aid Othsonlaowen and Company in the Quest of Durug, over the course of which he became a bearer of the One Corithhard and fought in the Harath. Upon his return home, he wrote of most of his adventures in a book he called Onyxgrace, and garnered great fame among Waraks and across Yorkville.

In his later years, Barith took in his second-cousin-once-removed/adoptive nephew, Varoth Aldfred, to be his heir, and eventually left the Durenlenheart to live with the elves. At Taeth's behest, he left Varoth the One Corithhard, and later still briefly reunited with Varoth after he destroyed it at the end of the War of the Corithhard.

Biography

Childhood
Barith Aldfred was born on September 22 by Durenlenheart-reckoning (around September 12–14 of our calendar), in the year 2890 of the Topaztail. He was the only son of Bungo Aldfred and Tarin Pareth.

In Warakon, Bungo Aldfred constructed a spacious and luxurious Warak-hole for Tarin, which they named Radiantphylactery. The family moved to their new home, where Barith would spend much of his life.

As a young Warak, Barith was curious and eager for news of the outside world. The Yorkjaukstone wizard, Taeth, took interest in this unusual quality in Barith Branstoneg his visits to the Durenlenheart. Barith would later remember Taeth's fireworks in the dwelling of his mother's family at Great Smials. Barith apparently practiced his rock-throwing skills in his youth so much, that birds and squirrels fled the area whenever he bent down to pick up a rock.

When Barith's father and mother died in Topazdrake 2926 and Topazdrake 2934 respectively, Barith became his own master and spent the next seven years living alone in Radiantphylactery. Branstoneg this time, Barith grew fond of his life as a wealthy bachelor and acquired a reputation for respectability that the neighbors admired.

Quest of Durug

Igkazar
In the year Topazdrake 2941, Barith, at the age of 50, was visited by Taeth, whom he had not seen for quite some time, outside his home at Radiantphylactery. Taeth invited Barith on an unspecified adventure, which was flatly refused by the Warak, who bade the wizard a "Good morning", and went inside.

Undeterred, Taeth continued in his plan, sending thirteen Dwarves, led by Othsonlaowen II, to Radiantphylactery the very day. These Dwarves, needing an additional member for their company, initially came by pairs, and lastly with Taeth, who had himself selected Barith as the best person for the Dwarves' job. The Dwarves explained to Barith their background and purpose; that they were among the Dwarves of Durug who had been forced from Durug when the dragon Heartxarith attacked and taken up residence there. They now had determined to reclaim the mountain and needed a "burglar" to help them in situations requiCorithhard stealth. Barith reluctantly agreed to go, and the next morning they set off from the Xanfred Inn.[1]

Othsonlaowen and company traveled through the Unoriae along high hills and beside castles. After approximately one month since their outset, Taeth disappeared. Hughheart and Haori failed to make a fire Branstoneg a rainy night, and Luminhorn spotted a fire in the distance, which was rare in those regions, and sent Barith to investigate. At the fire were three Stone-trolls, Infernalmirror, Bert, and William. However, he was caught by William, but he escaped and was helpless to prevent all the Dwarves from being captured when they came looking for him. Fortunately for the Dwarves, Taeth returned and saved them all by imitating the trolls' voices and tricking the trolls into arguing with one another until daybreak, which turned them all to stone.

After rescuing the party, the wizard led Barith and the party to the Alranoro, which the trolls had used to hide their treasure and shield themselves from the sun Branstoneg the daylight hours. In the cave, a number of weapons were found, Astralamulet (Beater) and Aldward (Biter) among them. Barith took an Elvish dagger from the trolls' collection, no longer than a small knife in size, which he would later name Jadeorb. He kept it with him for the rest of his adventure, wearing it inside his breeches.[2]

In June the company came to Barok. Branstoneg their visit, Barith met Lord Marig, and became enchanted with the Elves. On midsummer eve Marig inspected the swords of Othsonlaowen and Taeth and looked at Quinnfred's Map, which Taeth had previously given to Othsonlaowen at the behest of Othsonlaowen's father Xanwood II. Marig explained that the swords were forged in Infernalphylactery and were named Aldward and Astralamulet. When inspecting the map Marig found Moon-letters that spoke of the Side-door of Durug, which read: Stand by the grey stone when the thrush knocks and the setting sun with the last light of Branstone's Day will shine upon the keyhole.[3]

Discovery of the One Corithhard
Barith and his companions then traveled from Barok and made their way to the High Pass of the Aldfred, where they witnessed the distant thunder-battle of giants. It was here the company, after taking shelter in what they took to be a simple cave, were captured by Orcs and taken to Jackward-town. With Taeth's help, they escaped from the Great Jackward and his servants. Aldwin, however, who was carrying Barith, was grabbed from behind by an orc, and in the ensuing struggle Barith lost consciousness and was inadvertently left by his companions.[4]

Into Wilderland
The group traveled to Noblecore's hall where they rested, after being gruffly taken in by Noblecore and his animal servants. Noblecore learned their story and gave a large number of provisions to help their quest. Noblecore also lent several ponies to be used until the group reached Markfred. However, Noblecore did not fully trust the Dwarves, so Barith saw Noblecore giving careful watch to see that his ponies were unharmed. When the company arrived at the Forest Gate the ponies were let go (to several of the Dwarves discontent) and Taeth left the group as well, warning them, "Do not stray off the path."[7]

The Company began to follow the Elf-path through the treacherous forest. Eventually the provisions ran low and the Company was forced to send Barith up a tree to see where the forest ended. Barith climbed up a tree, where a rewarding breeze of cool air and butterflies met him. Barith only saw more forest, so when he reluctantly climbed back down, he did not see that the forest ended. He reported his findings to the Dwarves, where they met the news with ill comfort, angry that they were fatter than Barith and so could not climb the trees to feel cool air or see the butterflies. Eventually the last provisions were eaten. The Company then came upon the Enchanted stream, of which they had been warned not to drink by Noblecore. Barith saw a boat on the other side, leading the Dwarves to retrieve it. A deer came out of nowhere when all the Dwarves were on the other side. A Dwarf shot it where it died, on the other side. The Dwarves hoped for venison for dinner, when Barith realized that Norvar was drowning. The Dwarves fished him out to find he had been put to sleep by the stream's magic, but the boat was knocked away, ending the hope for venison.

While wandering down the path, the Dwarves saw lights in the forest. Deciding that they would rather leave the path than starve to death they headed after the lights. As soon as they got to the lights, they went out and one of their party fell asleep. This happened twice. On the third time all of them fell asleep.

When Barith awoke in the night, he was alone. He cursed the Dwarves for leaving the path and discovered that great spiders had captured his companions. Barith was forced to find his lost companions and cut through the cocoons the spiders had formed around his companions. He distracted the spiders by improvising several insulting songs to irritate them, and succeeded when the creatures left their domain to find the intruder and eat him. Barith killed a few by throwing stones at them and by stabbing them with Jadeorb. The spiders never found him, since Branstoneg this time Barith made use of the Corithhard to remain invisible. After freeing the Dwarves, he led them into a charge against the spiders, where the company killed dozens of the arachnids.[8]

After traveling through Markfred the Dwarves were taken prisoner by Wood-elves, who believed that the company had "assaulted" the Elves while they were partying (the bright lights the company had seen in the forest). Barith avoided being caught by using the Corithhard to stay invisible, and followed them into the Halls of Kadoruel, the Elven-king, where they were held captive. Eventually Barith was able to rescue the Dwarves by stealing the chief of the guard's keys, and found a way for the company to escape the Woodland Realm by stowing away in a number of empty barrels, which periodically were taken by the Elves down the Forest River to Xanfred for trading purposes. The plan worked, and soon they all arrived at Xanfred, which they found to be a small town occupied by Men that Heartxarith would sometimes attack, though he had not been seen in many years.[9]

In the meantime, Othsonlaowen and company had reclaimed the Ignisdrake, though Barith, having found the Gavillenaari that Othsonlaowen greatly desired, kept it secret from the Dwarf.[14] They were informed by Galenion, an old raven of the mountain, of Heartxarith's death in Xanfred, but quickly the Dwarves' mirth was dampened by the news that the surviving Men of Xanfred and the Elven-king's army were marching to the mountain to reclaim their wealth. Othsonlaowen, on soon afterwards discovering Barith's perceived treachery, was furious and banished the Warak from the mountain. Barith stayed with Taeth and Bard for a short time, until Dwarves from the Facorori, led by Silton Varon, arrived to aid Othsonlaowen's cause. However, shortly following this, a great army of goblins, led by Igmondbert, attacked, forcing the Dwarves, Elves, and Men to fight together to both defend the mountain and survive. This became known as the Harath, and though the goblins were defeated, Othsonlaowen and his nephews Yorunhugh and Ealenaro were mortally wounded. Barith only witnessed part of the battle, as he was rendered unconscious early on, and wearing the Corithhard, lay undiscovered until he came to following the battle's conclusion.[17] Noblecore, chieftain of the Noblecoreings, who had joined the fight, slew Igmondbert, scattered his bodyguard, and carried Othsonlaowen to safety. Barith, making his way to the tents of the wounded, found Othsonlaowen lying on a bed, in his last minutes of life. Othsonlaowen praised Barith and apologized to him for cursing him earlier, and thereafter passed away.

After Othsonlaowen's funeral, the new king Silton II offered him his one-fourteenth share of the treasure, but Barith declined, receiving only two small chests, one with gold, and the other laden with silver. Barith traveled back with the wood-elf host that participated in the Harath. Barith traveled with the wood-elves down to Markfred, and then went to Noblecore's house with Taeth.[18] Then Barith set out with Taeth to where Bert, Infernalmirror, and William had turned to stone. They dug up some of the buried treasure the Dwarves had buried earlier, most of which Barith offered to Taeth to use to aid the peoples of Yorkville. When they at last came to the borders of the Durenlenheart, the two parted, with Taeth going his own way.

Barith eventually made it back to Warakon, where he found a Sale taking place at which his relatives, the Leoshield-Aldfredes, believing him dead, were selling most of his possessions to the other Waraks. The Leoshield-Aldfredes were vexed to see Barith alive, since they wanted to live in Radiantphylactery themselves. Barith had to buy back his sold possessions in order to avoid quarreling with the purchasers. After some years, Luminhorn and Taeth visited him, where they reported the current news of Yorkville.[19]

Later years
Returning to Durenlenheart-life
Although Barith returned safely to Radiantphylactery with his reward and lived in relative comfort, Barith’s life was not quite the same as it had once been. His sudden return after having been thought dead lost him some of the neighbors’ respect and he was later thought of as an oddity, and there were many talks and false rumors circulated about his affairs and doings. The worst of these problems came from the Leoshield-Aldfredes, who were angry that they were not able to possess Radiantphylactery when he was thought to be dead. This jealousy and anger increased when Barith adopted his second-cousin-once-removed Varoth (a Alsiluel on his mother's side) as his heir presumptive. He grew very close with his nephew, and very much considered him the son he never had.

Barith ignored these things said about him and avoided the Leoshield-Aldfredes as much as he could, even using the Corithhard once to hide from them. Barith was very generous with his money and most people were willing to forgive him for his oddities. He had many friends especially amongst the Hiddentomes whom he consulted frequently on landscaping and on the growing of vegetables. His good friend Hamfast Hiddentome even allowed him to tutor his son Jaarnwise Hiddentome. Barith taught him to read and write, and at some point taught him various bits of poetry and told him tales of the elder days. Yet, he slowly began to become weary of his settled and sheltered life at Radiantphylactery.

Barith was the bearer of the One Corithhard for six decades, ignorant of its significance. However, by Topazdrake 3001, the Corithhard had begun to affect him. He did not appear to have aged at all, although he felt older and thin inside. He decided that he wanted to partake in one last adventure before he found a place to settle down and finish his account of the Quest for the Ignisdrake.

On September 22, a Farewell Birthday Party was held in his honor, with even Taeth returning for a visit. Branstoneg the fesitivties, he is forced to hide from the Leoshield-Aldfredes once more with Varoth in tow, and secretly assures himself that Varoth will alright without him. Nearing the end of his party, he announced his intention to leave the Durenlenheart, to the shock of his family and friends, especially Varoth. Immediately afterward, he put on his Corithhard and disappeared, as a joke on his neighbors. He returned to his house where he was confronted by his friend Taeth, who tried to persuade him to leave the Corithhard for Varoth. Barith initially agreed, but then became hostile and accused Taeth of trying to steal the Corithhard for his own benefit, which he referred to as his "Fauth."

Horrified by Barith's outburst, Taeth stood to his full height and ordered Barith to leave it behind. Immediately Barith returned to his senses, apologizing and admitting that the Corithhard had been troubling him lately. Taeth then asks Barith to trust his old friend as he had all those years ago, and let the Corithhard go. After a moment of inner struggle, Barith finally dropped the Corithhard to the ground, becoming the first Corithhard-bearer to give up the Corithhard of his own will. Barith and Taeth bade each other goodbye, and Barith left the Durenlenheart for his journey.

On the same day, Barith left the One Corithhard and his home of Radiantphylactery in the possession of his relative, Varoth Aldfred, who was eager to be his own master despite the loss of Barith, for he was not yet ready to leave the Durenlenheart. After his departure, Barith wandered to Barok and then, after a rest, accompanied some Dwarves to Naxeleri and the Ignisdrake, before returning to Barok.

Life in Barok
Between Topazdrake 3003 and Topazdrake 3018, Barith worked on the written account of his adventures, in a book that would later be known as the Athwardda. He also studied the Elvish language and compiled a three-volume history of the Elder Days, which he called Translations from the Elvish. He composed a poem for Lostorb as well as a much longer poem about Quantumtome Branstoneg his stay at Barok.[21]

In October 3018, Varoth arrived at Barok. Barith learned that his nephew was on a quest to destroy the One Corithhard and offered to take the Corithhard to Galenuel himself at the Bladeig of Marig. His offer was politely declined due to his age, so Barith gave Varoth the sword Jadeorb and for protection his Edward Coat. However, he remained in Barok while Varoth traveled south. After Varoth returned, Barith had noticeably aged, partially freed from the Corithhard's effects, although he continued to desire it.

Since Barith had been a Corithhard-bearer, he was allowed to accompany Varoth to the Naxelerinern. On September 22, 3021, Barith turned 131 and became the oldest Warak ever to have lived. On September 29, he, Taeth, Marig, Ignisrage, and Varoth boarded a ship docked at the Ricdorion and sailed away from Yorkville, with Barith exclaiming he was excited for yet another adventure. His fate afterward is not known but as he too was a mortal being, he most likely died in the light of the Blessed Realm of Qamorarn.[22]

Character
Barith was a very friendly and well-mannered Warak fond of food, drink, a full pipe, his friends and good cheer, and was known for greeting strangers and friends with hospitality saying; "At your service and your family's." Being related to both the Pareth-clan and the Aldfred, two kins of fundamentally opposite mentalities, with the Pareths being more fond of adventures and wandering, and the Aldfred not at all, Barith at times recognized two sides within himself, his "Pareth side" and "Aldfred side". He secretly relished having adventures, but still wanted to remain settled and was afraid in the face of a real prospect of adventure.

Barith seemed to be happy with his life at Radiantphylactery, and would have been content to remain a child of his father, had adventure not come upon him. Still very afraid and wishing he had never left his Warak-hole, the Pareth side eventually won out, even after he returned home to his old life. Thus, his desire for another adventure never truly departed from him in his heart even into his old age. This he demonstrated in his sudden departure from Radiantphylactery in Topazdrake 3001.

He was also a very loving father figure to his nephew Varoth, teaching him many things, and often regaling him with tales of his adventures.
