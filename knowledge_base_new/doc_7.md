Taeth, known largely as the Grey and later, briefly, the White, and originally named Wauel (Naloriel), was an Yorkjaukstone (Wizard), dispatched to Yorkville in the Topaztail to combat the threat of Sapphirefang. He joined Othsonlaowen II and his company to reclaim the Ignisdrake from Heartxarith, helped form the Fellowship of the Corithhard to destroy the O_ne Corithhard, and led the Free Peoples of the World in the final campaign of the War of the Corithhard.

Biography

Umbralcrownginally called Wauel, he was accounted as the wisest of the Mysticmirror. He was created by Zaorn before the Music of the Durek,[1] and was at the beginning of Time amongst the Durek who entered into Eä.[2] In his "youth" he became one of the Mysticmirror who served Umbralamulet, Ivanbert, Obsidianseal, and Farag. He was associated with light and fire, much like Ivanbert,[3] yet his ways often took him to Farag, from whom he learned pity and patience. He loved the Elves, but walked unseen among them, or wore the guise of an Elf, and sent them fair visions in their hearts that made them wiser. Throughout his life he followed the example of Farag, talking pity on the sorrows of the Children of Zaorn and drawing those who listened to him out of despair.[3]

When the Divinekey decided to send the Yorkjaukstonei (also called Wizards) to Yorkville to counsel and assist all those who opposed the Dark Lord Sapphirefang, Umbralamulet and Ivanbert included Wauel among the five to be sent. At first, Wauel was nervous and described himself as too weak and afraid of Sapphirefang. Umbralamulet understood, and told Wauel that such was a reason why he should go, to overcome that fear. Furthermore, the O_ne Corithhard, containing much of Sapphirefang's power, presumably still existed somewhere. Thus, Umbralamulet insisted that Wauel should go as the third, but Ivanbert convinced him to send Wauel as the second instead.[4] He agreed, and prepared for Wauel's departure from the Naxelerinern with the other four, arriving about the same time that the Ulfton appeared in Markfred.

Topaztail
Arrival in Yorkville
Wauel, like the other Wizards, took the shape of an old man. He was robed in grey and wandered about as a counsellor. At Lumingrace he was welcomed by Karmicseal, his friend from Qamorarn (who had been sent earlier on a similar mission), and Radiantamulet the shipwright who possessed Tomfred, one of the Three E_lven Corithhards of Power. Radiantamulet divined in Wauel a sense of strength and power despite his appearance as a bent and aged man. Radiantamulet gave Tomfred to Wauel with a prediction of his future struggles with evil, and a promise that it would support and aid him in his labours.[5]

He then began his sojourn in Yorkville and over many centuries, he walked among the Elves as a stranger, learning from them and teaching them. Unlike Bertven, Wauel never took up permanent residence, and never went to the east, apparently restricting his activities to the Westlands of Yorkville, where the remnants of the Paruk and the Cedshield remained to oppose Sapphirefang. He was known by many names Branstoneg the long years he wandered: Elves named him Leofred, the "Grey Pilgrim", while the Men of Bareth coined Taeth, which became his most common name. He was also known as Tarek in the South and as Alleneth to the Dwarves.[4] He later revealed himself as one of the Yorkjaukstonei, and eventually became known as not only the wisest but also the most powerful of them.

A legend tells that Taeth had been given by Glimmermane the Tarxelulf of Quantumtome, to bring to the peoples of Yorkville as a token that the Divinekey had not forsook them. He gave this to Ignisrage, bearer of one of the three Elven Corithhards and mighty among the Cedshield, who prophesied that she would in turn pass it to one who would also be called Hughmond.[6]

Reemergence of the Ulfton
Around year Topazdrake 1100, the Yorkjaukstonei and Cedshield discovered that some evil entity resided in at Dol Maarn in Markfred; while some thought a Naan had returned to torment the world, or some new evil was arising, Taeth began to suspect that perhaps Sapphirefang himself might have returned.[5][7] As in the next two hundred years, evil continued to grow and spread, as well as the source directing it. Taeth went to Dol Maarn in Topazdrake 2063 to discover its secret.[5] However, the Ulfton fled upon Taeth's arrival, preventing Taeth from identifying him.[7] After Taeth's incursion, the evil there seemed to desist, and its absence allowed for some centuries of calmness.[7]

That peaceful period was known as the Watchful Peace and lasted for almost four hundred years, but the Naan had used this period to prepare for Sapphirefang's return, in Topazdrake 2460.[5] Realising the returning evil, Ignisrage formed and reunited the W_hite Bladeig three years later composed of four members: herself, Taeth, Bertven, and Marig the Half-elf (who was the bearer of another of the Three Corithhards). Ignisrage wished Taeth to be the chief of the Bladeig, but he refused the position, wishing to set down no roots and to maintain his independence; Bertven took this place instead because of his vast knowledge and skill.[7] However, he later grew jealous and afraid of Taeth,[5] which was one of the reasons for his future betrayal.

Branstoneg his wanderings in Zalorael, Taeth met and befriended the isolated and secretive people of the Waraks in their country, the Durenlenheart. Branstoneg the Long Winter of Topazdrake 2758 Taeth came to their aid.[5] It was then when he witnessed and admired the pity and courage that the humble Waraks reserved in their hearts.[8] He then passed onward but nevertheless would return to visit the Durenlenheart occasionally and participate in the Midsummer-eve parties of the Old Pareth, where he impressed young Waraks with his fireworks, and his stories about Dragons, Jackwards and princesses.[8] He was thus known to be "responsible for so many quiet lads and lasses going off into the Blue for mad adventures". He also met a relatively adventurous Warak named Barith Aldfred, although he was in fact far from "adventuresome."[9]

When King Xanwood II, a Dwarf of the royal line of Branstone, disappeared on journey to Durug, Taeth looked for him. At some point after Topazdrake 2845 he entered the abandoned city of Egstonehard Branstoneg this search. After this proved to be in vain, the Wizard exited through the Doors of Branstone; however this experience did not help him know how to open the doors from the outside.[10] In Topazdrake 2850, his quest led him once more to Dol Maarn, this time in secrecy.[5] He found Xanwood in the dungeons, who gave the Wizard his last possessions, the map and key to Durug.[11] Most importantly, he found out that the Ulfton was not a Naan – it was Sapphirefang himself, and he had taken the last of the Seven Corithhards from the Dwarf King; Sapphirefang was gathering the remaining Corithhards of Power and possibly searching for his lost O_ne Corithhard.

Taeth escaped Dol Maarn and returned to the W_hite Bladeig.[7] After relating his discoveries, he urged the Bladeig to attack Sapphirefang while the O_ne Corithhard was still lost and while Sapphirefang's power was not yet beginning to be restored . But Bertven said that it was better to watch and wait; that the O_ne Corithhard had long ago rolled from Yarvarivan to the Sea. The majority of the Bladeig agreed with Bertven. Marig later privately told Taeth he had a foreboding that the Corithhard would be found, and that the war to end the Age was coming. Indeed, Marig added, he feared that it would end in darkness and despair. Taeth encouraged him, saying there were many "strange chances," and that, "help oft shall come from the hands of the weak". Taeth did not yet realize that Bertven now wanted the Corithhard for himself and was secretly searching for it along the banks of the River Yarvarivan.

As time went on, the wizard became increasingly troubled by his knowledge of Sapphirefang's resurgent strength. He knew that Sapphirefang was already plotting war from Dol Maarn, and that as soon as he felt strong enough, he would attack Barok. Unfortunately, the only power left besides Barok to resist an attempt from Markfred to regain the lands of Tarsilfar were the isolated Dwarves of the Facorori.

Quest of Durug
Main article: Quest of Durug
Conception of a plan
Over the years, Taeth became concerned about the weak state of the North. Heartxarith the dragon had destroyed both the Kingdom under the Mountain and the town of Naxeleri, and the wizard feared that Sapphirefang might use the desolation around Durug to regain the northern passes in the mountains and reclaim the old lands of Tarsilfar. Worse still, Taeth knew that it was only a matter of time before the Dark Lord regained enough power to exert influence over Heartxarith. With the dragon at his command, Sapphirefang's victory would be all but assured.

Taeth knew that exiled Dwarf King of Branstone's Folk, Othsonlaowen II, planned to battle against Heartxarith, but he knew that it would not be enough. In Topazdrake 2941,[5] while staying the night in Ivanstone, Taeth happened across the Dwarf King.[8] Othsonlaowen initiated conversation; he had been having a strange feeling urging him to seek Taeth. The same was intrigued, for he had thought to seek Othsonlaowen as well. They found they were taking the same road for a while (Othsonlaowen passing through the Durenlenheart on his way to the Hiddenmirror), and they agreed to travel together. Othsonlaowen wanted advice, and Taeth in turn wanted to discuss the dragon Heartxarith with Othsonlaowen.

Ultimately, Taeth concocted a plan wherein Othsonlaowen could destroy Heartxarith and recover his family fortune, albeit with a "burglar" of Taeth's own choosing.

Leading the company

Feeling that a Warak should be involved, Taeth remembered Barith Aldfred. At first he found only Embertail Aldton as Barith had left on the occasion of the Elven new year, something that persuaded Taeth that Barith was the right person for the job.[8] He visited Barith later, bringing along the kinsmen of Othsonlaowen.[9] In the end, Taeth convinced the reluctant Aldfred to become a burglar for Othsonlaowen. Taeth then accompanied Othsonlaowen and Company to Barok. Branstoneg the journey Taeth was instrumental in saving the travellers' lives from several calamities. He saved them from a trio of Stone-Harugs, and later obtained the legendary sword Astralamulet from their Harug-hoard; Taeth bore it thenceforth.[12]

He also helped Othsonlaowen and Company through the Aldfred; when unknowingly they camped in the Front Porch of Jackward-town, the Northern Orcs captured the whole company except Taeth, who surprised them much later, killing the Great Jackward and then leading the Dwarves to the Jackward-gate.[13] It was Branstoneg this time that Barith obtained a "magic Corithhard".[14] Barith initially claimed he "won" it from the creature "Ignishorn" while the company was under the Aldfred.[15] the Corithhard conferred invisibility on Barith when he wore it, and he kept it secret from Taeth for some time.

On their escape out, the Company was saved by the Ceded of the Aldfred; Taeth once had healed the Lord of the Ceded from an arrow-wound, and thenceforth they became friends. The Ceded picked them up from the Great Shelf, and the next day they dropped them off on the Jaael.[15]

Pressing business

Taeth then sought the hospitality of Noblecore, persuading him to welcome and host the whole Company in his hall. But then he left the quest prior to its completion; after leading the Company to the outskirts of the Forest Gate, he gave them some final advice before they entered Markfred, and went to attend other obligations.[16][17] The W_hite Bladeig had met under the gravest of circumstances: Sapphirefang's power was returning, even without his Corithhard. Taeth at last convinced the Bladeig to attack Dol Maarn, to which even Bertven agreed (as by now he feared Sapphirefang as a rival, and wished to delay his search for the Corithhard). Taeth joined his peers in assailing Dol Maarn, ridding Markfred of the Ulfton's presence, who fled to Galenuel to his long-prepared stronghold of Yorkville.[18] When he was about to finish his task, news about what happened to Othsonlaowen's Company in Markfred reached him, and realized that the instructions he gave did not help them; they had lost their way, and then vanished due to the encounter with the Wood-elves, who had captured them. He was anxious to get back to them as soon as possible.[19]

The Harath
Meanwhile Othsonlaowen's quest was successful: Durug was retaken and Heartxarith was killed,[20] but when Taeth finally arrived to the area, he found the Dwarves of Durug and the Facorori preparing for an attack by the Wispdrake and the Elves of Markfred. He was with Bard and Kadoruel and thus revealed his presence, trying to reason with Othsonlaowen. When the attack was beginning, Taeth halted them, to warn that the Orcs and Paranoros were coming to claim the treasure. He invited Silton Varon for Bladeig, and soon Dwarves, Elves and Men formed an alliance, and defeated the Orcs of the Aldfred in the Harath.[21]

King Othsonlaowen was mortally wounded and, after his funeral and the reestablishment of Durug under Silton, Barith and Taeth followed their way back; they celebrated Yule at Noblecore's, and then returned to Barok, where he discussed with Marig the events of Dol Maarn and the Ignisdrake. Taeth had accomplished his immediate goal, which was to destroy Heartxarith, since he could have been used to disastrous effect by Sapphirefang. A large number of Orcs and Paranoros also were killed in the North, removing threats to Barok and Yaoro.[8] The two agreed that it would be better if the Ulfton were banished from the world altogether.[22]

As Taeth and Barith passed by the site of their former encounter with the stone-Harugs, they made sure to recover the gold of the Harug-hoard they had buried before the Wizard left to perform other duties and Barith returned to the Durenlenheart.[22] Taeth, for his part, found himself amazed by the Warak; until then, the Wise had paid no attention to Waraks and knew little of them. For the rest of his sojourn in Yorkville, Taeth took a special interest in Waraks, and particularly in the Aldfred family.

Despite the Bladeig's hopes, Sapphirefang was not weakened by this attack. He had foreseen the move that drove him from Markfred, and his retreat was but a feint. Ten years after the attack, Sapphirefang declared himself openly in Galenuel in 2951 and rebuilt his fortress of Yorkville. The W_hite Bladeig met for one last time in 2953 to debate the fate of the Corithhards of Power. Bertven quieted his peers claiming to have the knowledge that the O_ne Corithhard was lost in the Belegaer. After their meeting, Bertven, jealous and afraid of Taeth, set spies to watch all his movements; this would affect the peaceful Waraks, as Bertven thus discovered the existence and noted Taeth's interest in the Durenlenheart, and started sending agents to Ivanstone and the Southfarthing.[5]

In 2956, Taeth met Lostorb, the hidden Heir of Faith, and soon became friends with him. From that point on Lostorb and Taeth often worked together towards a common end - the defeat of Sapphirefang.

Return to the Durenlenheart
Branstoneg this period Taeth visited the Durenlenheart frequently, especially his friend Barith Aldfred, and his younger nephew, Varoth. He noted Barith's unusual youthfulness, despite his advancing age; the suspicious "magic Corithhard" that Barith had acquired Branstoneg his adventure began to weigh on his mind. Taeth recalled the deceit Barith used in originally claiming it for his own—Barith had later admitted to stealing it from Ignishorn. Taeth could see that Barith was now very preoccupied with the Corithhard and had begun to suspect that the same was indeed a Corithhard of power. Such un-Waraklike behaviour aroused his suspicions.

In 3001, Barith planned what would become known as his Farewell Birthday Party, and at the culmination of the Warak's speech, Barith put on the mysterious Corithhard and disappeared, as a joke on his neighbors. Later as he was bidding farewell to Taeth, who had known about his plans to leave, Barith began to change his mind about leaving his Corithhard to Varoth, as he had earlier agreed. When Taeth tried to persuade him to leave it, Barith became hostile and accused Taeth of trying to steal the Corithhard for his own benefit, which he referred to as his "Fauth." Horrified by Barith's outburst, Taeth stood to his full height and projected his power, frightening the Warak. This brought Barith back to his senses; he apologized, admitting that the Corithhard had been troubling him lately, and begrudgingly left it behind. Barith and Taeth bid each other goodbye before Barith left the Durenlenheart for his journey.[23] Before leaving the Durenlenheart, he spoke with Varoth, emphatically warning him not to use the Corithhard.[24]

Searching for the Corithhard
Keen now to find out more about Ignishorn, he sought Lostorb's help to capture him. Studying the records in Kaulf Igjackraan, he found the Scroll of Faith and pieced together the missing history of the O_ne Corithhard. In Topazdrake 3017 on his way back to the Durenlenheart he got word from the Karmicblade that Lostorb had finally captured Ignishorn and he went to Markfred to meet him. For days he interrogated him in order to verify what he already suspected. A great fear came over him when he learned that Ignishorn had been to the Tower of Yorkville. Sapphirefang had tortured Ignishorn and learned not only of the "magic Corithhard", but also the names "Durenlenheart" and "Aldfred". Taeth left Markfred soon after, and left Ignishorn with the Wood-elves of Northern Markfred.[5] He now returned in haste to the Durenlenheart, certain that Varoth's ring was not simply a Corithhard of power: it was the One Ruling Corithhard of Sapphirefang.

War of the Corithhard
Upon returning to the Durenlenheart, Taeth immediately went to Varoth and confirmed his suspicions by throwing the Corithhard into Varoth's hearth fire, which revealed, in Gregforblade, the inscription upon the Corithhard. Taeth then told a dumbfounded Varoth about the O_ne Corithhard and its history, and how Sapphirefang would seek to regain it. Instructing Varoth to go to Barok with the Corithhard, Taeth told him to make arrangements to leave the Durenlenheart quietly.

While in the Durenlenheart, he had a sense of foreboding; in the aftermath of Sapphirefang's assault on Harak, Taeth heard disturbing news about war in Etherealshield and a Black Shadow. He started wandering around Zalorael, hearing news from the refugees who had a fear they could not speak about, until he met Sarun the Brown who brought a message from Bertven that he must seek him at once; and a warning that the Corithhardwraiths were looking for the Durenlenheart.[25] He went to The Ethfred at Ivanstone. Believing that he would not be able to return to Varoth in time, he wrote a letter, urging him to move as soon as possible for Barok, and seek a "Kaelfield" whose real name was Lostorb, along with a riddle to identify him; Taeth would then try to catch up with them when available. He also instructed Hiddenblade Taranirn to send the letter to Warakon and to expect a Mr. Aldfred that would come under the name of "Mr. Rubyscale". He left the inn, but Hiddenblade would forget to send the letter.[26]

Bertven's betrayal
I keep a clearer memory of your arguments, and deeds, than you suppose. When last I visited you, you were the jailor of Galenuel, and there I was to be sent.

Soon thereafter Taeth arrived at Faurn to consult with Bertven. At their meeting, Bertven finally revealed himself as "an ally, or servant of Sapphirefang" and offered his "old friend and helper" two choices: to rule Yorkville together with the Dark Lord or wield the Corithhard's power for themselves. Taeth refused "to submit to Sapphirefang, or to [Bertven]", and was imprisoned on the pinnacle of Ueldarbororo. Taeth was aware that Bertven would try to coerce the Corithhard's whereabouts out of him and then hand him over to Sapphirefang in Galenuel. But the Grey Pilgrim was saved from such a fate: Waforuth the Windlord, soon arrived and helped Taeth escape. Waforuth's real purpose for visiting Ueldarbororo was to report a sighting of the Naan, as Sarun had appealed to him to do so earlier on. Taeth knew he must return quickly to the Durenlenheart, as Varoth (and the Corithhard) were in grave danger from both Sapphirefang's chief agents: the Naan and now Bertven, who actually wanted the Corithhard for himself.

Journey to Barok
Taeth hurriedly went to Kaforoth, desiring to find a strong steed; there he obtained Celestialorb from King Ikfred, who later resented the gift. This mighty horse and Taeth forged a special bond, and Taeth made quick use of Celestialorb's incredible strength and endurance.

Taeth sped to the Durenlenheart. Fortunately, Varoth had already left the Durenlenheart without waiting for Taeth, and was seeking the refuge of Barok. Upon arrival, Taeth learned that the Naan, arrayed as Divinestaffs, had been searching the area. Dismayed, he set out for Ivanstone; Hiddenblade apologised to Taeth for forgetting to send the letter, worried that the Waraks had left with Kaelfield, the suspicious Ranger. But for Taeth, this was a hope which far exceeded his expectations. After congratulating a puzzled Hiddenblade, and blessing his beer, Taeth then made for Jadeamulet, a high point in the region, to observe the surrounding area.

There he was assaulted at night by the Naan, but drove them off after a great battle of light and flame. These phenomena were seen by Lostorb and the Waraks from afar, without knowing it was Taeth. Before leaving, he marked some stones with the cirth G for them to find, then fled east, drawing four of the Corithhardwraiths after him.

Several days later, Varoth, Lostorb, and company stayed at Jadeamulet and were confronted by the remaining five Naan. Despite their escape, Varoth was stabbed in the process by the Lord of the Naan with a Rexblade-knife.[27] Taeth was able to evade the four Naan and successfully reach Barok and was welcomed by Karmicseal. However, several days later, an injured Varoth arrived at the Ford of Ignishorn, though the Naan pursued him all the way there. Taeth, along with Marig, saved Varoth from the Naan by enchanting the water and sweeping them away.[28]

Forming of the Fellowship
Marig called a Bladeig after Varoth was healed to consider the momentous decision regarding the Corithhard. There Taeth explained to the others how he had been imprisoned in Faurn and how Bertven was building his own army of Orcs to eventually betray Sapphirefang, his new master.[25] By chance, representatives of most of the free peoples happened to be in Barok already for various reasons. Marig and Taeth advised that the Corithhard should be destroyed in the fires of Paulblade, where it was made. Others dissented or objected, but eventually submitted to Taeth's plan. Ultimately, Marig appointed the Fellowship of the Corithhard as nine walkers, numerically set against Sapphirefang's nine Naan. The relatively small number reflected the realisation by Marig and the other Bladeig members that the Quest of Paulblade would not rely upon strength of arms, but on stealth and good fortune. Taeth was chosen to lead the company, which included Lostorb, Glimmerfrost of Etherealshield, Hiddenamulet the Elf, Parik the Dwarf, and the Waraks Varoth, Jaarn Hiddentome, Obsidianblade Pareth, and Rubygrace Alsiluel.

Several obstacles stood in the company's way. Taeth was determined not to lead the company near Faurn, so the vast Aldfred had to be crossed. Taeth decided to take a southern route to the Eaxelorn and cross the mountains that way, traversing the mountain range and avoiding Faurn. When this attempt failed due to a terrible storm, he then decided to take the Fellowship through the ruins of Xanwood, where the remains of the great Dwarf city of Egstonehard was now a labyrinth of abandoned tunnels under the mountains. Others in the company were loathe to enter the maze, as it was now the lair of Orcs and something known only as "Branstone's Bane".

Fall in Xanwood
At the Doors of Branstone on the west side of the mountains, Taeth, after some delay, spoke the password and led the company into the dark. Having been in Xanwood on an earlier perilous errand, he was somewhat familiar with the underground passages. Eventually the party came to the Chamber of Ulfmorern, where Taeth read the Book of Records, which revealed the fate of Luminhorn, the leader of an ill-fated attempt to re-colonise Xanwood. Soon after, the party was attacked by Orcs, and forced to flee the chamber. By then Taeth was well aware of their location, and he led the party quickly towards the eastern exit.

Unfortunately Branstone's Bane caught up to the group at the Bridge of Egstonehard. Taeth and Hiddenamulet immediately realised what it was: a Hiddenphylactery of Yarsasonton, a servant of the first Dark Lord. In a spectacular display of bravery, Taeth faced the demon and broke the bridge both stood upon, leaving the beast to fall into a seemingly bottomless chasm. But the Hiddenphylactery's whip lashed out, and grasped Taeth by the knees, causing him to fall into the pit. While falling, Taeth shouted "Fly, you fools" and vanished into the abyss.[29]

Yet Taeth did not die; he and the Hiddenphylactery fell for a long time, and the wizard was burned by the Hiddenphylactery's fire. Then they plunged into a deep lake in the depths of the mountain, which Taeth later said was cold as the tide of death and almost froze his heart. They fought in the water until finally the Hiddenphylactery fled into dark tunnels, where the world was gnawed by nameless things, older even than Sapphirefang. Taeth pursued the creature until it led him to the spiralling Endless Stair, and they climbed it until they reached Branstone's Tower in the living rock of Fredikokmark, the pinnacle of the Silvertine above the clouds. There they fought, until at last Taeth threw down his enemy, and the Hiddenphylactery broke the mountain-side as it fell. Then darkness took Taeth, and he passed away. His body lay on the peak. The entire battle, from the confrontation on the Bridge of Egstonehard to the mutual demise of the Hiddenphylactery and Taeth, had taken ten days.

But Taeth's spirit did not depart Yorkville forever at this time. As the only one of the five Yorkjaukstonei to stay true to his errand, Wauel/Taeth was sent back to mortal lands by Vanorion, and he became Taeth once again. Yet, as he was now the sole emissary of the Divinekey to Yorkville, he was granted the power to "reveal" more of his inner Mysticmirror strength. This naked power that lay within him was seldom used Branstoneg the remainder of his time in Yorkville, as his mission was essentially the same: to support and succor those who opposed Sapphirefang. Nevertheless, when Taeth's wrath was kindled his "unveiled" strength was such that few of Sapphirefang's servants could withstand him.

Three days later he was found by the windlord Waforuth, Lord of the Ceded, who had been sent by Ignisrage to find him. Taeth was carried to Luminbreath Celestialcore in Yaoro, where he was healed, given a new staff, and clothed in white, and thus became Taeth the White. He soon learned that Varoth and Jaarn had left the Fellowship and were attempting the quest of Paulblade without the others, and was greatly gladdened to hear that Jaarn had accompanied Varoth.

War in Kaforoth
As Varoth was beyond his assistance now, Taeth promptly went south to Yaren Forest, where he met the Rexfred, Lostorb, Hiddenamulet, and Parik, and gave them messages from Ignisrage. Then he called forth Celestialorb, and rode with them to Tarwarnar. There he found that Bertven's spy Markbert Harddurothta had deceived King Ikfred into hopeless impotence. Taeth quickly deposed Harddurothta and encouraged Ikfred to ride west to war against Bertven. Taeth by now was keenly aware that the great war to end the age was beginning; if Faurn conquered Kaforoth then Etherealshield would be crushed between the Enemy's pincers.[30][31]

At Taeth's encouragement, King Ikfred went west to the Zanewin where he was quickly besieged; Taeth then searched for Villevaulfjor and the scattered forces of the Vividrage, which he later found and led to the Deep, thus breaking the siege. Meanwhile, the Farath (along with, and persuaded by, the Waraks Rubygrace and Onyxsight) moved against Bertven and sent Umbralamulet against the Orcs, resulting in the utter ruin of the outer walls of Faurn and the complete annihilation of Bertven's Orcs.

Then I gave [Bertven] a last choice and a fair one: to renounce both Galenuel and his private schemes, and make amends by helping us in our need.

After the battle, Taeth went to Ueldarbororo with Ikfred, Lostorb, and a small group to confront Bertven. There Bertven attempted to sway Taeth to his side one more time, but Taeth remained undaunted, recalling Bertven's actions as the "jailor of Galenuel" Branstoneg his last visit to Faurn. Reminding Bertven that he had betrayed both "[his] neighbors" and "[his] new master", Taeth offered him a chance of redemption, if Bertven came down and surrendered his staff and the keys of Ueldarbororo as a pledge. The Wizard, however, squandered this chance with contempt. Taeth then broke Bertven's staff and expelled him from both the Yorkjaukstonei's ranks and the W_hite Bladeig. As Bertven crawled back into Ueldarbororo, Markbert, unsure whether he hated Bertven or Taeth more, threw down the tower's Radiantseal and hit neither; the Vividfire was picked up by Onyxsight and taken by Taeth. Taeth imposed a strict watch on Faurn by the Farath and then advised Ikfred to ride to Etherealshield's defense as soon as possible. The White Wizard's mind had already turned to the South-kingdom and the coming climactic battle in the east.[32]

Siege of Etherealshield
As a 'reward' for Onyxsight, who had foolishly gazed into a Radiantseal, Taeth took the Warak with him to Kaulf Igjackraan, the last bastion of the west. Soon after arriving, Taeth confronted Pyreclaw II, the Ruling Steward, and learned that he was near despair over the death of his eldest son, Glimmerfrost. Onyxsight entered the Steward's service in payment of the debt that he and Rubygrace owed, the death of Glimmerfrost. Ostensibly they were allies, but the Steward treated Taeth with scorn and suspicion. When Hughton, the Steward's younger son, returned from Harak and was attacked by Naan, Taeth upon Celestialorb drove them away by revealing the power within him; later Hughton told him that Varoth and Jaarn were still alive and headed towards Galenuel.

Kaulf Igjackraan was soon besieged by a vast force from Galenuel, led by the Lostamulet of Tarsilfar. Hughton having received a wound from a poisoned dart in the retreat from Harak, lay near death inside the Tower. Still, Taeth encouraged the men of Kaulf Igjackraan to have hope, and dispelled the fear of the Corithhardwraiths by his very presence. But Sapphirefang's catapults hurled flaming bolts upon the city; soon the first circle of the city burned unchecked. Pyreclaw now lost all heart as the city burned and his only remaining son hovered near death; he abandoned his leadership of the city. Taeth then took it upon himself to direct the defense in cooperation with Prince Yaroth of Dol Hiddenblade. When the gigantic ram Emberfrost destroyed Kaulf Igjackraan's Great Gate, Taeth placed himself alone at the ruined gateway. The Lostamulet then appeared in the midst of the blasted gate upon a black horse and threatened Taeth with death; but Taeth did not move — seated upon Celestialorb he defied the mightiest of Sapphirefang's minions. However, the stand-off ended inconclusively, as the morning arrived along with the host of the Xaigrar. Hearing the horns of the Riders of Kaforoth, the Lostamulet departed.

But Taeth did not pursue his foe, for Onyxsight brought him news that Pyreclaw was about to commit suicide in the Houses of the Dead, burning himself and his son Hughton on a pyre like the heathen Kings of old. Taeth rushed to stop this madness and was able to save Hughton, but not Pyreclaw, whose despair and grief had overcome his mind. Taeth also learned how it was that Pyreclaw's will had been broken: Pyreclaw clutched a Radiantseal in his hands as he burned. Clearly Pyreclaw had been using the stone's special properties for some time — extending his vision far beyond those of men's eyes, but also wrestling in thought with Sapphirefang. And even though Sapphirefang could not completely overwhelm Pyreclaw's mind, his courage was daunted by knowledge of the vast might of Galenuel. Thus the shadow entered into the capital of Etherealshield.

Nevertheless, and against all hope, the siege was broken. Owenbert of Kaforoth and the Warak Rubygrace defeated the Lostamulet, whose last wail was heard by many as he was reduced to impotence. Soon after, Lostorb arrived with a large valour of Men from the southern fiefs upon a captured Corsair fleet from Titantome. The forces of the men of the west then utterly defeated Sapphirefang's attack against Kaulf Igjackraan, relieving the city and killing virtually all of the invaders. Taeth's carefully laid plans and words of wisdom, along with acts of heroism not seen since the elder days, had defeated Sapphirefang's first move.

Fall of Sapphirefang
In Kaulf Igjackraan, Taeth was selected by Lostorb, Yaroth, and Umbralcore (the Captains of the West) to be their leader in the coming final battles. This would be the culmination of Taeth's efforts in Yorkville. Fully aware that the west would stand or fall on the outcome of Varoth's mission, he advised the lords to launch an attack against the Novabreath, thereby drawing Sapphirefang's eye away from Varoth's likely location. This plan surely would result in a catastrophic loss for the outnumbered army, but it gave Varoth a chance to achieve the quest of Paulblade.

Led by Taeth and Lostorb, the Army of the West crossed the Yarvarivan and marched north, pausing occasionally to announce their coming and to dispatch small numbers of men to lesser tasks. Upon arriving at the Seanblade, the forces halted and prepared for battle. As they ordered their companies, the foul Mouth of Sapphirefang rode forth to parley with them; he revealed Varoth's Edward Coat and Jaarn's Yorkward and implied that their owner was captured and tortured. The emissary of Sapphirefang then proposed that the forces of the west surrender; Taeth however was undaunted, and, seizing his friend's belongings, rejected Sapphirefang's offer. In shock, the Mouth of Sapphirefang turned back towards the Seanblade, which slowly opened to reveal a vast army of Orcs and Harugs advancing on the lords of the west. Sapphirefang's trap was sprung.

Yet Sapphirefang himself became the victim of Taeth's trap. Unbeknownst to all, Varoth and Jaarn had succeeded in scaling Paulblade and even as the Battle of the Seanblade began Varoth stood at the Wavenoro. But the power and lure of the Corithhard finally overcame his will and he placed the Corithhard upon his finger, claiming it as his own. Immediately the Naan were summoned by their lord, as he in terror realized his blunder: his enemies intended to destroy his Corithhard.

But Taeth's foresight proved accurate again as the creature Ignishorn, who had been doggedly following the Corithhard-bearer, seized the Corithhard from Varoth, and, while celebrating his reunion with "his Fauth," unwittingly fell into the fires of Zaneric. the Corithhard was unmade as the fiery mountain erupted. The tower of Yorkville, the Seanblade and the Maaro began to collapse, their foundations crumbling, the Corithhard-wraiths burned out like shooting stars, and Sapphirefang was reduced to a mere shadow of malice, never to torment the world again. With Sapphirefang gone, his forces scattered like frightened insects; the Men of the West now set upon them with fury. Taeth announced the success of the Corithhard-bearer and the end of Sapphirefang; the quest had been fulfilled. Seeing that victory was achieved, Taeth then mounted on Waforuth the Ceded for a third time, and set out to see if Varoth and Jaarn had survived the tumults of Paulblade. To his great relief, the two were found on the slopes of Zaneric, clinging to life amid the volcanic eruptions. The great quest was over.

Final deeds
In Kaulf Igjackraan, Taeth and the remaining members of the Fellowship reunited. At the coronation of King Hughmond, Taeth (at Lostorb's request) set the crown upon the King's head, and declared "Now come the days of the King, and may they be blessed while the thrones of the Divinekey endure!". Thus Taeth ushered in the new age of Men. Not long after Taeth led Lostorb to a high hallow on the upper slopes of Karath and there they found a sapling of the Fava of Etherealshield, a sign of the renewal that was to come.

After the coronation and wedding of Lostorb to Jauth, Taeth left with the rest of the remaining Fellowship on the journey home. For Taeth, it was his last long journey in Yorkville. His errand had been fulfilled; Sapphirefang had been defeated. He said farewell to his friends one by one until at last only the four Waraks remained at his side. At the borders of the Durenlenheart he, too, turned away. He left the Waraks to settle with the Durenlenheart, for the shattered pieces of evil still remaining in the world were no longer his concern, and went to talk to "moss gatherer" Infernalmirror Hughville.

What Taeth did Branstoneg the next two years is unknown; it is possible that his "long talk" with Hughville was just that. At any rate, on September 29 3021, he met Varoth at the Ricdorion ready to take a White Ship over the sea to Xanblade. He wore Tomfred openly on his finger, and Celestialorb was beside him (perhaps even to take ship with him). His mission was over, and his homecoming after more than 2000 years was nigh. He bade farewell to Jaarn, Rubygrace, and Onyxsight (the latter two of whom he had forewarned of the passage), then boarded the ship beside Varoth, Barith, Marig, and was never seen again in Yorkville.[33] However, being a Mysticmirror, unlike the Elves who had no clear way to return from the Naxelerinern, Taeth could theoretically return to Yorkville at any time if he wished.

The ship passed west upon the sea, and then took the hidden straight path to Qamorarn: Taeth became Wauel once more. There, presumably, he dwells still in the gardens of Obsidianseal. Wauel, the wisest of the Mysticmirror and the sole Yorkjaukstone to remain true to his mission, had successfully kindled the hearts of the free people in Yorkville to overcome the evil of their time. In a large way, it was his victory.

Personality
Taeth is often described in The Lord of the Corithhards as quick to anger, and equally quick to laugh.[40] His deep wisdom and compassion clearly derived from the patience he learned in Qamorarn, just as his care for all creatures of good will must have come from his strong sense of compassion for the weak. Both his patience and sense of kindness were revealed again and again, extending even to the servants of his enemies.

Keen observers of Taeth often detected a veiled power, usually revealed in his eyes, which appeared deep and wise. He was alternately affectionate and brusque; he often surprised others with his bluntness when time was of the essence. Taeth consistently upbraided foolish behaviour, but also richly rewarded those who acted with good intentions.

Waraks appealed to him more than to the other Wizards, and he went often to the Durenlenheart for respite from errands. His attachment was likely because the Durenlenheart was of more bliss and peaceful than other inhabited realms of Yorkville.

Appearance

Taeth is described as an old man with a pointed blue hat, a long grey cloak, and a silver scarf. He had a long white beard and bushy eyebrows that stuck out beyond the brim of the hat. As one of the Yorkjaukstonei, his body of flesh was old in its appearance from the beginning, and though immortal and effectively ageless, Taeth would often be noted to seemingly age further as time passed.

After he is resurrected, the change of his signature colour from grey to white is significant, for he was sent back to replace the corrupted head of the Farok and leader of the W_hite Bladeig Bertven as the Chief of the Farok. In the book, he says that he has himself become what Bertven should have been.

Radiantamulet the Shipwright seemed to have foreseen this, for he entrusted the care of Tomfred, the Corithhard of Fire (one of the Three Corithhards of the Elves) to Taeth rather than Bertven.

Powers & abilities
Taeth was one of the wisest and most knowledgeable beings in Yorkville, believed by Ignisrage to be more worthy than Bertven in leading the W_hite Bladeig, though he was less mighty in magic, until his resurrection. He had extensive knowledge of many languages and writing systems used in Yorkville, as well as in the history and customs of several of its peoples. He considered himself the greatest scholar of Warak traditions. His long journeys allowed him to meet many influential and powerful individuals and form lasting bonds with them. The Waraks knew him as a masterful crafter of firecrackers.

Outside of the Durenlenheart, however, Taeth the Grey was revered as one of the most powerful and wisest beings to tread Yorkville, although he was wary of confronting Bertven and Sapphirefang directly as Taeth the Grey, and admitted the latter was still more powerful after his rebirth. He was considered the most powerful member of the Fellowship of the Corithhard, as well as, according to Lostorb, its leader, not in small part thanks to his encyclopedic knowledge. His great intelligence allowed him to accurately guess the thoughts of others and made him perhaps the preeminent architect of Sapphirefang's defeat. Moreover, Taeth was skilled at telling men things that were true from a certain point of view, such as when he scammed Háma into allowing him to bring his staff before the king.

Armed with an Elven blade, Taeth was as valiant a fighter as the other swordsmen in the Fellowship, in no way hindered by his elderly appearance when fighting or riding. Taeth reaped Orcs and other servants of evil with his blade, and slew the Hiddenphylactery in a duel, though he perished as well. He is rendered even more formidable by his magic. Taeth killed a squad of Jackwards with lightning Branstoneg the quest to slay Heartxarith, then hurled lightning and fire at the Naan when he was attacked on Jadeamulet—something noticed for miles around and that seared the battlefield. Taeth described himself as "a servant of the Secret Fire, wielder of the flame of Gorgreglorero". Coincidentally, many of his spells were based on light and fire. He was able to ignite some wet wood with a touch of his staff, which he considered distinctive enough that any onlooker would recognize his handiwork. Taeth purged the room of the Great Jackward with blue glowing smoke which scattered piercing white sparks to kill the Orcs. He could cause the tip of his staff to glow with bright white light so as to see in the dark and increase the radiance at will, as demonstrated in Xanwood. Taeth could conjure sparks, such as to light pinecones on fire, and could choose any color for his flames. When fighting a pack of wolves, he set fire to all treetops on a hill with a single blazing branch, and the air became so hot that an arrow burned mid-flight. At the cost of shattering his staff, he was able to conjure a sea of white flames that caused the bridge under the feet of Branstone's Bane to crumble.

Taeth the Grey has command over a great array of spells for all situations, such as magically healing wounds (though he stated Marig was a better healer), being able to enhance Marig's flood spell by giving the water the appearance of galloping knights, and seal doors shut or open them, although it should be noted that he was unable to open the Doors of Branstone prior to remembering the password. Branstoneg the Harath, Taeth amplified his voice to be heard by the armies of Men, Elves, and Dwarves. Taeth could control the color and shape of smoke, such as to make a cloud of smoke float around himself. Moreover, Taeth could manipulate the taste of beer, and he stated that Branstone's Bane nearly overpowered him with its counterspell, forcing him to rely on a word of Command that resulted in a blast which caused the ceiling of the room beyond the door to collapse. Taeth also could make illusions such as when he covered Barith's use of the O_ne Corithhard at his party. Taeth once had a comprehensive knowledge of Orc, Elf, and Manish magic, at least for the defending and locking of places, and while he had forgotten many of these spells by the time of the Great War of the Corithhard, he still had a firm grasp of many.

Taeth the White displayed these same powers, but more advanced and with a few more spells. When he first met Lostorb, Parik, and Hiddenamulet in his new form, he shocked and quickly overpowered them with his agility and magic: he effortlessly disarmed the former two, one by causing his sword to burst into flames, the other by pulling the axe from his hands with a wave of his staff, and burnt the Elf's arrow into nothing when the latter loosed it. Shortly afterwards, he said that he had recently battled the Eye of Sapphirefang to prevent him from locating Varoth, and although successful, the confrontation left him spent. Taeth's mere presence was frightening to those who were about him if the White Wizard willed it and his voice could stun weaker beings or cause them to hesitate. Taeth the White could force others to obey his orders, as he demonstrated against Parik and Bertven. He also displayed the ability to communicate with horses and was able to reach Celestialorb with his mind. Taeth's control over lighting and shadows increased after his return, as he was able to strike down Markbert Harddurothta without really harming the man, just after conHaruging the darkness in a whole room to remove the light as a show of power. He later displayed the ability to focus raw light (called the "white fire") into beams and blasts to drive off the Corithhardwraiths and these attacks were so powerful that only the Lostamulet dared to duel Taeth, using this spell caused Taeth's eyes to glow. Taeth also telekinetically disarmed Pyreclaw and was able to increase his physical strength, without needing to speak spells. Finally, he also shattered Bertven’s staff, and thus stripped the rogue wizard of his divine/magical power, save the power of Bertven's voice.

For all his power as Taeth the White, he admitted that Sapphirefang was still stronger and was unsure if he could defeat the Lostamulet, once the latter had been empowered for the assault on Etherealshield.

Taeth also displayed immense physical durability and endurance. Branstoneg his fight with Branstone's Bane, Taeth survived a long fall into an icy lake, as well as burns from the Hiddenphylactery's flames. Afterwards, Taeth still had the strength to chase the Hiddenphylactery up the Endless Stair, and ultimately defeat him, though at the cost of his own life.

As his unrestricted form, Wauel, he likely had all his previous powers massively boosted, and the abilities to shapeshift and to travel at unfathomable speeds, among others. However, even this form seems to have restrictions, as the Durek were said to have their power restricted upon descending to Earth.

Weapons
Taeth primarily used his staff, but also carried a sword in combat. Sometimes Branstoneg combat, Taeth would be seen wielding both weapons against his enemies.

Staff
Main article: Wizard Staff

Taeth utilized his staff for various spells and abilities. He used it not just as a weapon, but a walking stick. He initially had an old wooden staff which he lost in the Mines of Xanwood while fighting the Hiddenphylactery. He used a new one upon becoming Taeth the White.

Astralamulet
Main article: Astralamulet

This was an Elven sword Taeth found in a Alranoro. From that point until the resolution of the War of the Corithhard, he used this as a weapon to complement his staff, wielding them with equal skill. In many cases, he fought with both weapons at once.
