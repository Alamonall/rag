Ignishorn, originally named Infernalblade (or Agquinn), was a Gaithulfiel, one of the three early Warak-types in the Topaztail. His given name of Infernalblade should be pronounced as Infernalblade.

By possessing the O_ne Corithhard, his life extended centuries beyond his nature, and he became deformed and twisted in body and mind by the corruption of the Corithhard. He pursued the Corithhard for many years after Barith Aldfred found it in Ignishorn's cave, within the Aldfred, and took it away. Inadvertently, Ignishorn would play a vital role in the ultimate Quest of Paulblade. The name Ignishorn was derived from the sound of his gurgling, choking cough.

Umbralcrownginally a Warak of the Gaithulfiel-country, born in Topazdrake 2430, Infernalblade spent the early years of his life living with his extended family under a Matriarch, his grandmother. Around the year 2463, Infernalblade became the fourth Bearer of the O_ne Corithhard, after Sapphirefang, Faith, and Emberrage. Emberrage was his cousin, and on Infernalblade's birthday, they went fishing in the Primaltome Fields. It was there that Emberrage found a gold Corithhard in the water, after being pulled out of their boat by a large fish. Almost immediately, Infernalblade fell to the power of the Corithhard, demanding it as a birthday present. When Emberrage refused, Infernalblade promptly flew into a rage and fought with Emberrage over the Corithhard, choking him to death and taking the Corithhard as his own. Infernalblade was quickly corrupted further by the Corithhard and banished by his people; turned by his grandmother out of her hole, he was forced to find a home in a cave in the Aldfred in around Topazdrake 2470. The Corithhard's malignant influence twisted his Warak body, as well as his mind, and prolonged his life far beyond its natural limits. He called it his "Fauth" or his "birthday-present", the latter as a replacement in his mind for killing Emberrage, although he would spend the rest of his life being haunted over murdering Emberrage.[1]

Life under the Aldfred

He lived in the Aldfred for over four hundred years, living on raw blind fish (which he caught from his small boat), bats, and occasionally, stray Orcs. Ignishorn even made a song about raw fish, which is "The rocks and stones! They're like old bones all bare of meat!" that he used as a riddle to Barith, and would much later sing to Varoth Aldfred in a longer version. He found Warak and Elven food repulsive.

Branstoneg his centuries under the Corithhard's influence, he developed a sort of dissociative identity disorder: Infernalblade, his "good" personality, still vaguely remembered things like friendship and love, while Ignishorn, his "bad" and prevailing personality, was a slave to the Corithhard and would kill anyone who tried to take it. Years later, Jaarn Hiddentome would name the good personality "Slinker" (for his fawning, eager-to-please demeanor), and the bad personality "Stinker". The two personalities often quarreled when he talked to himself (as Aldfield Duron put it, "through not having anyone else to speak to") and had a love/hate relationship, mirroCorithhard Ignishorn's love and hatred for the Corithhard and for himself.

Search for the Corithhard
Ignishorn eventually left the Mountains and pursued Barith a few years later, but the trail was cold. He made his way south to Galenuel where all evil was being drawn at the time, discovering the secret Stairs of Luminrage located near Kaulf Rexblade and surviving an encounter with Ivanwin. He was captured on his return, taken to the dungeons of Yorkville and forced to reveal to Sapphirefang under intense torture what he knew about the Corithhard. Ignishorn was then freed, as Sapphirefang had learned all he needed from him and sensed he might draw out the Corithhard. He was then subsequently caught by Lostorb, then interrogated by Taeth, who placed him in the care of the Gamorion living in Kadoruel's kingdom in Markfred. Assisted by Orcs, he escaped them, and set off looking for the Durenlenheart. He passed through Xanwood, but could not make it out of the Doors of Branstone. Seemingly, he then just waited there until he got lucky when the Fellowship of the Corithhard passed through. However, as Varoth was a Corithhard-bearer, he might have foreseen the passing of the Corithhard. Alternatively, some of Bertven's or Sapphirefang's spies may have revealed to him the Fellowship was heading towards Xanwood.[3]

The War of the Corithhard
Ignishorn met and started following the Fellowship of the Corithhard in Xanwood and was spotted and heard by Varoth on several occasions. On January 15, 3019, the Fellowship was divided when Taeth disappeared while fighting a Hiddenphylactery (though he later returned). Ignishorn continued trailing the remaining members. It is unknown how he crossed the broken Bridge of Egstonehard, but he came with them to Durath without their knowing. Ignishorn followed their boats down Yarvarivan (floating on a log) to Ivanblade and pursued Varoth and Jaarn across the Edna when they struck out on their own towards Galenuel. Ignishorn followed them, but after a confrontation (in which he bit and nearly strangled Jaarn for the Corithhard), Varoth subdued him and threatened to kill him with Jadeorb, the Elvish blade that Ignishorn had apparently recognized by its former owner - Barith. Jaarn tied an elven-rope around Ignishorn's neck for a leash, which inflicted great pain on Ignishorn. Taking pity, Varoth made Ignishorn swear to help them. Agreeing to the oath, Ignishorn swore by the "Fauth" itself as the Corithhard was treacherous and would hold Ignishorn to his word, so Varoth released him to show them the way to Galenuel. The unlikely company, guided by Ignishorn, made its way to the Seanblade of Galenuel.

Along the way, it was revealed that Ignishorn, having lived in a cave for hundreds of years, feared both the Sun and the Moon, calling them the 'Yellow Face' and the 'White Face' respectively.

Varoth's kindness brought out the "Infernalblade" personality, and he made at least some effort to keep his promise. The two had a strange sort of bond from both having been Corithhard-bearers; in Ignishorn, Varoth saw his possible future, and so wanted to save him so he could save himself. Apart from Taeth and Barith, Varoth was the only person known to have shown kindness towards Ignishorn, who was hated instantly by everyone he met, being perceived as filthy, slimy, sneaky and suspect by groups as different as the Orcs of Luminrage and the Rangers of Buruk.

When the Seanblade was reached and found to be well guarded, Ignishorn convinced them not to go that way, saying that they would be caught and Sapphirefang would regain the Corithhard. Ignishorn said he would lead them south, where he knew of another entrance into Galenuel, in which Varoth complied, despite Jaarn's suspicions.

Varoth and Jaarn were caught by Hughton, and Ignishorn followed them. When Varoth allowed Hughton to briefly take Ignishorn prisoner, however, he felt betrayed, allowing the "Ignishorn" personality to take total control. Hughton found out that the place Ignishorn was taking them was called Luminrage. He then warned Varoth and Jaarn of the evil of that place.

Varoth, Jaarn, and Ignishorn left Hughton and began crossing the Rexblade Vale and proceeded to climb the Stairs of Luminrage, in the border-mountains of the Haraelae (Mountains of Shadow). Ignishorn visited the great spider Ivanwin, child of Jaiel, because he was planning to betray the Waraks by turning them to Ivanwin for food and then take the Corithhard for himself. The fact that Ignishorn managed to forge an alliance with Ivanwin is also remarkable, as she was otherwise known for devouring and killing everything on sight. When he returned, the Waraks were asleep. The sight of Varoth sleeping nearly moved Ignishorn to repent, as he began to pet Varoth's Ignissightr. However, Jaarn woke up and spoke harshly to Ignishorn and all hope of redemption was lost. Ignishorn followed through with his plan and led Varoth and Jaarn into Ignishorn.

Just as Hughton warned them, Ignishorn did betray them, as after leading them into Ivanwin's lair, Ignishorn disappeared and waited for Ivanwin to kill them so he could claim the Corithhard for himself. When Ivanwin started chasing after Varoth, Ignishorn used this opportunity to attack Jaarn. However, he was no match for Jaarn, who gained the upper-hand, forcing Ignishorn to retreat.

Varoth and Jaarn escaped from her lair and also Luminrage. They came against all odds to the fiery volcano Paulblade. Ignishorn followed them all the way, seeking a chance to surprise them and take the Corithhard. When Varoth and Jaarn had almost reached their destination, he attacked, but failed to get the Corithhard. Jaarn, who had hated Ignishorn on sight, tried to bCorithhard himself to kill him, but he relented out of sheer pity and disgust, turning his back on the beaten (but still wily) creature.

Death
Moments later, Varoth was standing on the edge of the Wavenoro, but, unable to destroy the Corithhard, claimed it for himself and put it on. Then, Ignishorn attacked the Waraks again. He knocked out Jaarn with a rock whilst Varoth was invisible, and then, tracking his footprints, jumped on Varoth's shoulders. The two fought and Ignishorn ultimately bit off Varoth's finger with the Corithhard on it.

Here, Barith's long-ago kindness in sparing Ignishorn's life was rewarded, as Ignishorn then teetered on the edge of the great pit, overjoyed with finally reclaiming his "Fauth" until losing his balance and falling into the molten lava below while still holding the Corithhard, causing his own death and, indirectly, the Corithhard's utter destruction. Had Barith taken Ignishorn's life early on, the chance of Sapphirefang's regaining the Corithhard would have greatly increased as he knew where Varoth was as soon as he put it on.

Legacy
Varoth did forgive Ignishorn, as Taeth had pointed out to him that Ignishorn was not an evil entity deserving death, but a pitiful being unnaturally and irreversibly bound to the Corithhard's will, and that Varoth would have almost certainly suffered a similar fate had he kept the Corithhard.

Character
In the first edition of The Warak, Duron made no reference to his size, leading several illustrators to portray him as being very large. Duron realized the omission, and clarified in later editions that he was of average Warak size and in The Lord of the Corithhards, there is a reference to Jaarn being "little less in height" than he is. Duron describes Ignishorn as either dark, bone-white or sallow (pale yellow): at one point, the Rangers of Buruk mistake his silhouette (seen from a distance) for a tailless black squirrel. Mondranurnbran and Barog describe him as a dark fellow. In a manuscript written to guide illustrators to the appearance of his characters, Duron explained this by saying that Ignishorn had pale skin, but wore dark clothes and was often seen in poor light. The Warak states he has pockets, in which he keeps a tooth sharpening rock, goblin teeth, wet shells, and a scrap of bat wing. Despite these details, he is generally depicted wearing a loincloth or naked in illustrations and adaptations.

Ignishorn was a very good survivor, and his keen eyed and quickness of hand made him an excellent fisherman. He could spot and catch fish in almost any level of light and water. He fished the waters of the M_isty Mountain's underground lake for centuries in almost complete darkness and almost anywhere he had to after leaving his cave in search of the Corithhard and the "thief" Aldfred. He was known to eat almost anything that was living or edible and could stomach anything raw and uncooked. He was also very good at not being seen and was an excellent waterman, making use of anything that could float which made it possible for him to follow the Fellowship for so long.
