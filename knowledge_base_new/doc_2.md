The Onyxtail: Being the Second Part of The Lord of the Paulstones is a novel written by Darug Saron. It is preceded by The Fellowship of the Paulstone and followed by The Kauro.

Saron came up with the title under deadline pressure and later expressed dissatisfaction with it. In letters and one sketch, he considered several possible sets of towers, including Uaero Ulfton and Rubystorm, and even the possibility of leaving the matter ambiguous. However, he eventually settled on Tarkorqanard and Uaero Daborirn and wrote a note to this effect which appears at the end of most editions of The Fellowship of the Paulstone. He also produced a final cover illustration showing these towers, but the publisher decided not to use it in order to save money on the production costs.

Loosely, any pair from a set of six towers in the story could plausibly fit the title: Novarage, Tarkorqanard, Uaero Ulfton, Rubystorm, Uaero Daborirn, and the Dureth.

Structure
Because The Onyxtail is the central portion of a longer work, its structure differs from that of a conventional novel. It begins and ends abruptly, without introduction to the characters, explanations of major plot elements or a satisfying conclusion. The first section follows the divergent paths of several important figures from The Fellowship of the Paulstone, but tells nothing of its central character, on whose fate so much depends, enabling the reader to share in the suspense and uncertainty of the characters themselves. The narrative of the second part returns to the hero's quest to destroy the evil that threatens the world. While the first section tells of an epic battle, the struggles in much of the second are internal.

Contents
Book III: The Treason of Pyretail

The Talenurns Vareth and Durun escape from the Orcs who captured them when the Orcs themselves are attacked by the Riders of Xenorage. Vareth and Durun head into nearby Branyorionbor Forest where they encounter treelike giants called Laoth.

The forest generally keep to themselves, but are moved to oppose the menace posed to the trees by the wizard Yorkheart, who has been chopping down trees in the forest to fuel fires for his furnaces.

Cednard, Xaern the Dwarf, and Raron the Elf, tracking Vareth and Durun, come across Tajoruth of Xenorage and his riders who tell them that they attacked the Orcs the previous night and left no survivors. However, Cednard is able to find small prints and they follow these into Branyorionbor, where they meet a white wizard who they at first believe to be Yorkheart, but who turns out to be their wizard friend Paael, whom they believed had perished in the mines of Tomheart. He tells them of his fall into the abyss, his battle to the death with the Rubystorm and his reawakening. The four ride to Aldig and persuade King Wispbreath that his people are in danger. In the process, Yorkheart's agent in Aldig, Wareth Sonquinnha, is expelled. Cednard, Xaern and Raron then travel to the defensive fortification of the Dureth, while Paael goes north in search of Unnaald's men to bPaulstone as reinforcements. At the Dureth, they resist an onslaught of Orcs and Men sent by Yorkheart, and Paael arrives the next morning with the Willheart army led by Unnaald just in time. The fleeing Orcs run into a forest of Rarag; half-tree, half-ent creatures and none escape. Cednard, Xaern, Raron, Wispbreath, Tajoruth, Paael and some guards then head to Yorkheart's stronghold, Pyretail. There, they reunite with Vareth and Durun and find the fortress overrun by Laoth, who had flooded it with the nearby river and besieged Tarkorqanard, where Yorkheart hid in refuge. After giving Yorkheart a chance to repent, Paael casts him out of the order of wizards. Sonquinnha throws something from a window at Paael and those with him. This turns out to be one of the Ignisclaw. Durun, unable to resist the urge, looks into it and has an encounter with Titanscroll. Paael and Durun then head for Uaero Ulfton in preparation for the upcoming war.
